The Dual Challenge

Kenya faces two interconnected crises: 1.2 million educated youth unemployed while critical infrastructure projects lag behind schedule.

Youth Unemployment Crisis

Educated graduates unable to find meaningful work. Wasted human capital and growing social instability.

Infrastructure Delays

Roads, drainage, utilities consistently delayed due to fragmented labor supply and poor coordination.

"The WFU is not just a jobs program — it's a national infrastructure delivery mechanism."
Structural Unemployment vs Infrastructure Gap

How WFU Works

01

Training (3-6 Months)

KSh 5,000/month stipend + accommodation. 70% hands-on skills in 6 clusters: Road Construction, Utilities, Logistics.

02

Apprenticeship (2-3 Months)

KSh 15,000/month. Real project sites with professional supervision and performance assessment.

03

Full Deployment

KSh 30,000/month + bonuses. Organized teams delivering priority infrastructure projects nationwide.

Deployment Strategy

Direct Execution

WFU teams directly execute government & county projects under professional supervision.

Labor Partnerships

Trained teams supplied to vetted contractors on structured commercial agreements.
